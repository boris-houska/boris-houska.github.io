About#

Boris Houska is a Professor at ShanghaiTech University. He received a Diploma in Mathematics from the University of Heidelberg in 2007 and a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from KU Leuven in 2011. From 2012 to 2013, he was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Centre for Process Systems Engineering at Imperial College London. He subsequently held a Researcher position at Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2013, followed by Visiting Professor appointments at the Freiburg Institute for Advanced Studies and at the Institute for Microsystems Engineering at the University of Freiburg (both in 2014). He has also held several shorter visiting appointments, including at the University of California, Berkeley (Winter 2017) and Imperial College London (Summer 2018). In 2024, he was Visiting Professor at the IMT School for Advanced Studies Lucca.

Research#

Selected topics and publications:

Operator-Theoretic Control#

Nonlinear dynamics can be represented via linear evolution in infinite-dimensional function spaces using Koopman and Fokker–Planck operators. This enables the use of low-rank linear algebra and convex optimization tools for nonlinear system identification and control design.

📄 Convex operator-theoretic methods in stochastic control
📄 Towards global optimal control via Koopman lifts
🎥 Recorded presentation

Set-Theoretic Control#

Set-theoretic methods provide a geometric framework for representing uncertainty, constraints, and invariance in control systems. The main challenge is to obtain set representations that remain tractable in high dimensions while preserving structure required for control Lyapunov function and robust control design.

📄 Polyhedral control design: Theory and methods
📄 Intrinsic separation principles
📄 Configuration-constrained tube MPC
📄 Control Lyapunov function design via configuration-constrained polyhedral computing

Distributed Optimization and Control#

Distributed optimization decomposes large-scale control problems into smaller subproblems that can be solved in parallel across interconnected systems. Augmented Lagrangian methods such as ALADIN enable fast and reliable coordination for non-convex and structured optimization problems arising in control.

📄 An augmented Lagrangian based algorithm for distributed non-convex optimization
📄 Distributed optimization and control with ALADIN
📄 Towards distributed OPF using ALADIN
🎥 Recorded presentation

Global Optimization and Control#

Global optimization methods provide guarantees of optimality for nonlinear control problems with non-convex dynamics and constraints. Functional-analytic and relaxation-based approaches enable tractable formulations of otherwise intractable optimal control problems.

📄 Global optimization in Hilbert space
📄 Branch-and-lift algorithm for deterministic global optimization in nonlinear optimal control

Robust Optimization and Control#

Robust optimization accounts for uncertainty in dynamical systems by enforcing performance and constraint satisfaction under worst-case realizations. Convex and bilevel formulations enable tractable approximations of nonlinear robust optimal control problems.

📄 Robust optimization for MPC
📄 Nonlinear robust optimization via sequential convex bilevel programming

Optimal Control Software#

Efficient numerical methods are essential for solving optimal control problems in real time. Structure-exploiting algorithms and code generation techniques enable fast implementations for embedded and large-scale systems.

📄 ACADO Toolkit -- An open source framework for automatic control and dynamic optimization
📄 An auto-generated real-time iteration algorithm for nonlinear MPC in the microsecond range
